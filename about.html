About Me

Who am I?: Jacob Haselton

What did you want to be when you grew up?

When I was young, I wanted to be all the standard things: cop, firefighter, and soldier. However, as I grew up, I got more into motorcycles and now my dream is to run a motorcycle shop for custom built motorcycles.

How would you describe yourself as a child?

I was a good student, and I was picked on for it. I kept to myself until I got to high school where I finally found a group of friends I fit in with.

Did you have a nickname? How’d you get it?

Yes, I do have a nickname, Cyclops. I got it from my stepfather after I lost my right eye and it just kind of stuck with everyone since.

Who makes you laugh the most?

My wife makes me laugh the most. She just has a sense of humor that kills me.

Do you have any favorite stories from your marriage or about your spouse/partner?

One of my favorite memories of my wife is from our wedding ceremony. In the middle of it, she kissed me because she missed me, even though it wasn’t the traditional thing to do.

What inspired you to pursue this job?

I want to earn enough money to give my wife, myself, and any animals we have along the way a good life.

Describe the work that you do.

I work as a consultant in the oil and gas industry, helping to design gas processing plants and booster compressor stations. These plants remove impurities from natural gas to make it suitable for use.

What challenges have you faced in your career/passion, and how did you overcome them?

I came into a field that I had no experience in and I learned everything I could at work and even did some independent studying in order to become one of the more skilled engineers at my firm. I am still working to become better.

How do you stay motivated or inspired when things get tough?

I grew up with a low-income family and my drive no matter how bad it gets is to provide a better life for my family.

What advice would you give to your younger self?

Keep muddling through, it gets better.

What are you looking forward to in the future?

I want the American dream: owning a house with some land and having a happy family to share life’s experiences with.

How do you show thanks or appreciation?

Usually vocally, but I also perform acts of service if I can see something that needs doing that would make someone's life easier or better.

Cake or pie?

Pie
